FLORA AND FAUNA OF THE REGION

Askania-Nova

One of the most amazing places is the largest and oldest protected steppe in Europe –Askania-Nova, preserved in its original form. Bison, ostriches, rare Przewalski horses, zebras and many others live here.

The Straus-Yug

  • A unique private zoo, 15 minutes’ drive from Melitopol, has been delighting visitors for over 20 years.
  • The 8-hectare area is home to more than 30 species of animals and birds: in addition to ostriches, there are wolves, foxes, raccoons, llamas, Cameroon sheep, Romanov sheep, Przewalski's horses, donkeys, goats, a camel, nutria, squirrels, guinea pigs, rabbits and even a lion and a tiger.
  • The Straus-Yug Zoo is a powerful center of attraction for both Melitopol residents and the surrounding area, as well as guests of our city and region. The zoo owners are people with big hearts who love their pets very much.

FLORA AND FAUNA OF THE REGION

Biryuchiy Island

  • Biryuchy Island is a spit in the southern, widened part of Fedotova Spit, located in the western part of the Sea of Azov. Stretching 20 kilometers in length and reaching 5 kilometers in width at its widest part, it is an amazing place.
  • Once, before the 1920s, this piece of land was a full-fledged island, separated from the mainland by a narrow strait. Over time, under the influence of sediment and the expansion of the sand spit, the strait disappeared, and the island ceased to be isolated.
  • The origin of its name is quite interesting. In ancient times, the island was called "Wolf Island", and "Biryuk" is an obsolete word meaning a lone wolf, which has its roots in the Turkic "Boryu". They say that wolves were seen here in the old days, but there is no reliable evidence of their permanent habitation.
  • The nature of Biryuchy is unique. The local steppes are home to many rare plants listed in the Red Book.

In summer, this spit is a picturesque corner: on one side it is washed by the Sea of Azov, and on the other by the Utlyuk estuary. In August, the water temperature near the shore warms up to +30°C, which makes swimming especially pleasant. The sea here is shallow, with a flat bottom, safe for recreation

Among the attractions are:

  • Azov-Sivash National Nature Park, covering an area of 52,154 hectares.
  • The Biryuchiy lighthouse rises at the southwestern end of the spit.

GEOGRAPHY

Melitopol State University is located in a region where history and nature are intertwined.

Kamennaya Mogila (Stone Grave) 

The pearl of this heritage is the Kamennaya Mogila, a natural and historical monument located very close to Melitopol.

Kamennaya Mogila is not just a cluster of stones, it is a whole world of ancient culture and spirituality. There are 87 grottoes and caves, 65 of which contain thousands of petroglyphs - drawings and symbols left by our ancestors. This ancient temple holds many secrets and mysteries of the past.

Interesting fact: the Kamennaya Mogila is older than not only Stonehenge, but also the pyramids of Egypt, which makes it even more unique and significant.

On the territory of the Kamennaya Mogila, scientists discovered tools, household items and jewelry dating back to different historical eras, which confirms its important role in the lives of ancient peoples.

The Kamennaya Mogila is considered a sacred place for many peoples, including the Scythians and Turks. Rituals and ceremonies were held here, and many believe that this place has special energy.

On the stones of this historical monument, you can see ancient rock paintings – petroglyphs, which date back to the Bronze Age. These drawings are an invaluable source of information about the life and culture of our ancestors.

Melitopol State University recommends that everyone delve deeper into the study of the Stone Tomb and treat this unique historical and cultural monument with care. This is not only a valuable heritage, but also a source of inspiration for scientific research.

House of Ilya Adirovich

The most famous three-story building, built at the beginning of the 20th century, on A. Nevsky Street - today it houses the 8th building of MelSU, the Department of Music and Choreographic Education.

Interesting fact: it is known that the house was built as a result of a dispute between timber merchant Ilya Adirovich and merchant Ivanov Chernikov. They wanted to prove to each other whose house was better. Unfortunately, it is unknown who won the dispute.

What made this building stand out from other buildings was that it was built using white bricks instead of the usual red ones.

The place here was picturesque and, despite revolutions, wars and time, the house stands and continues to serve, preserving echoes of past times.

Spassky sand quarry

Spassky sand quarry (near the village of Terpenye) is one of the pearls of the Zaporozhye region.

A real miracle created by the joint efforts of nature and man can be called the Spassky sand quarry, which is rightfully considered one of the most beautiful natural attractions of the Zaporozhye region.

This quarry is located just 25 kilometers from Melitopol, between the villages of Terpenye and Troitskoye.

The most significant cuts are observed in the ravines that emerge from the right side into the Molochnaya River valley, especially in the vicinity of the villages of Troitskoye and Terpenie. At the very bottom lies a fairly thick layer (10 meters) of layered loose sands of a yellowish-bluish-white color with bright orange interlayers. These sands consist mainly of quartz grains and, with the exception of the very top layers, do not contain any fossils.

The age of the Spassky sand quarry is at least 5 million years. The sands were formed at a time when the local lands were either covered by advancing shallow seas or dried up almost completely.

Healing Springs Park

The power of the healing springs of the village of Terpenye is known throughout the Zaporozhye region.

Near Melitopol, in the small village of Terpenye, there is an amazing place, the park "Healing Springs". On the territory of the park there is a natural elevation from which five natural springs flow. Four of them flow into a lake, in which you can swim. The fifth, called "Nepokorny", flows in the opposite direction and disappears in the depths of the park.

Due to the rich mineral composition, the waters of the springs have unique healing properties, and each of them is different. Everyone who passes through here, and, of course, local residents collect water for drinking: it is valued for its healing properties.

Interestingly, depending on the season, the water in the lake can change color. Near the healing reservoir there is a healing bath, and an ancient oak tree more than 450 years old grows nearby.

The village of Terpenie in Zaporozhye region is located 20 km from Melitopol. You can get there by car or bus, and if you don’t have a car, you’ll have to walk about 5 kilometers from the village to the springs.

MelSU recommends that students and staff visit the Healing Springs Park as a unique place for relaxation and health improvement.
